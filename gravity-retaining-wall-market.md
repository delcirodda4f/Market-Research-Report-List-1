<p><p>A gravity retaining wall is a type of wall designed to hold back soil or rock from a vertical or near-vertical slope. These walls rely on their own weight to resist the lateral pressure exerted by the soil they retain, making them suitable for various applications in landscaping, road construction, and civil engineering. <p><strong>The Gravity Retaining Wall Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Oblique</li><li>Pitching</li><li>Upright</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Gravity retaining walls are categorized into three main types: oblique, pitching, and upright. </p><p>Oblique walls feature a slanted design that enhances stability by distributing lateral pressure more efficiently. Pitching walls have a gradually inclined surface, promoting drainage and reducing soil movement. Upright walls, on the other hand, stand vertically and rely on weight to resist external forces. Each type is selected based on site conditions, load requirements, and aesthetic preferences, ensuring effective soil retention and stability in various applications.</p></p>
