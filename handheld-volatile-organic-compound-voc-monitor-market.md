<p><ul><li>PID</li><li>Metal-oxide Semiconductor</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The handheld volatile organic compound (VOC) monitor market primarily consists of two types: Photoionization Detectors (PID) and Metal-Oxide Semiconductor (MOS) sensors. PID monitors measure VOCs by ionizing gas molecules using ultraviolet light, providing high sensitivity and real-time data, making them ideal for industrial and environmental applications. In contrast, MOS sensors detect VOCs through resistance changes in metal-oxide materials upon exposure to gases, offering cost-effective solutions suitable for indoor air quality monitoring and lower-concentration applications. Both technologies cater to diverse detection needs in various settings.</p></p>
