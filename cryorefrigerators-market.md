<p><p>The cryorefrigerators market is segmented into several types based on their operating principles. Gifford-McMahon cryorefrigerators utilize a two-phase helium cycle for low temperatures. Pulse-tube cryorefrigerators achieve cooling without moving parts, relying on gas oscillation. Stirling cryorefrigerators utilize a cyclic compression and expansion of gas for efficiency. Joule-Thomson cryorefrigerators exploit the Joule-Thomson effect for cooling. Brayton cryorefrigerators utilize a gas compression and expansion cycle. Additionally, "Other" categories may include emerging technologies and hybrid systems, broadening the market landscape.</p></p>
