Digital output sensors provide discrete signal levels, enabling easy integration with digital systems and offering improved noise immunity. They typically offer more precise measurements and straightforward data interpretation. In contrast, analog output sensors provide a continuous voltage or current signal proportional to the measured current, allowing for real-time monitoring and more straightforward integration into existing analog systems. Both outputs serve distinct applications based on sensitivity and integration needs.</p></p>
